Introduction

Air travel has seen many transformations over the decades. From the rise of in-flight entertainment, to heightened security measures from the effects of the 11 September 2001 attacks to the popularity of long-haul flights, these are some of the dynamic changes that the air travel industry faced, partly because of the changing passengers’ needs. The recent COVID-19 pandemic is of no difference. It has changed the passengers’ preference to flight experiences, with a much significant emphasis on health and safety. [1] This paradigm shift not only underscores the adaptability of the aviation industry but also raises questions about the future of travel in a world forever altered by the pandemic.

Motivation

The motivation behind this research endeavour lies in the profound impact that evolving passenger preferences have on the air travel industry and, ultimately, on the travel experiences of millions worldwide. With each passing year, the industry witnesses shift in priorities, influenced by factors ranging from technological advancements to global events like the COVID-19 pandemic. Understanding these shifts is vital for airlines, airports, and travellers alike. For airlines and airports, it means aligning their services and offerings with the evolving needs of their clientele. For travellers, it means making informed decisions that prioritize their safety, comfort, and overall satisfaction.

This research seeks to address this critical intersection between passenger preferences and the aviation industry's strategies. By providing a comprehensive visualization that unravels these shifts over time, we empower travellers with invaluable insights to make well-informed decisions and develop contingency plans when booking their next flight. In a world where air travel plays an increasingly integral role, this research serves as a beacon, guiding both industry stakeholders and passengers towards a more seamless and tailored travel experience.

Flight Delays by Continent

The dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of airline performance, flight delays across U.S. states, and temporal trends. In terms of airline performance, Southwest Airlines Co. leads with a significant 15.92% of the total flight delay count, followed closely by American Airlines Inc. and Delta Air Lines Inc. The flight delays across U.S. states reveal substantial variations, with Texas experiencing the highest delays at 42,559,790, while states like Delaware and Vermont show minimal delays.
The flight delays over time depict the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic, with a noticeable decline in 2020 and a subsequent increase in 2021 and 2022 as air travel resumed. Reasons for flight delays highlight carrier and late aircraft delays as prominent contributors, with fluctuations in weather and National Aviation System delays. Notably, the average carrier delay minutes declined in 2020, reflecting the pandemic's influence, but increased in 2021 and 2022 as travel demand rebounded. Overall, the dashboard offers a detailed perspective on airline operational performance, regional variations in delays, temporal trends, and the factors influencing flight delays in the United States.

Focusing on weather delays that are largely uncontrollable by the aircraft carriers, we can observe that majority of the airline carrier fall within a range of 0 to 10,000 minutes of delays a month, with a couple of the airlines displaying out of the norm delays from 18,000 to 28,000 minutes of delays a month. One relationship we can observe from this bubble chart is that as the number of delays increases, the longer the delays, in minutes, for that month. We can also observe that those with the higher number of delays tend to belong to the same group of airlines throughout the years. It is interesting to note that majority of the airlines were able to keep the number of delays, due to weather, at largely within the same range of one another, suggesting that they could have better planning compared to the carriers with constant large number of delays, or are located in locations with generally favourable weather conditions.

Conclusion

Based on our observation on the visualizations, our team identified the following potential opportunities for the aviation industry to improve their offerings and optimize future investment on services that has the greater impact on travellers’ experience and satisfaction.

Increase effort on maintenance and offerings for economy class flights during June and July period. We observed that during this period every year, there is a substantial increase in the number of economy class flights being sold by the airlines. From another visualization on travellers’ satisfaction, the team noted that travellers are least satisfied on short and medium haul economy class flights. With more travellers during the June and July period, if airlines improve their economy class offerings and quality during these periods, they can greatly improve a large number of travellers’ experience and vastly improve their overall ratings.

Create family packages for flights to popular destinations during the June to July period. We observed that during these periods, in addition to the increase in the number of economy class flights, the visualizations also indicated a substantial increase in family travellers during this period. By offering dedicated family packages, the airlines can simplify the booking process and also gain better control over standardizing in-flight services and amenities. This approach minimizes the need for individualized requests, thus enhancing efficiency and enabling airlines to provide a consistent, high-quality travel experience for the families.
