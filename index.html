Introduction

Air travel has seen many transformations over the decades. From the rise of in-flight entertainment, to heightened security measures from the effects of the 11 September 2001 attacks to the popularity of long-haul flights, these are some of the dynamic changes that the air travel industry faced, partly because of the changing passengers’ needs. The recent COVID-19 pandemic is of no difference. It has changed the passengers’ preference to flight experiences, with a much significant emphasis on health and safety.

This paradigm shift not only underscores the adaptability of the aviation industry but also raises questions about the future of travel in a world forever altered by the pandemic.

Motivation

The motivation behind this research endeavour lies in the profound impact that evolving passenger preferences have on the air travel industry and, ultimately, on the travel experiences of millions worldwide. With each passing year, the industry witnesses shift in priorities, influenced by factors ranging from technological advancements to global events like the COVID-19 pandemic. Understanding these shifts is vital for airlines, airports, and travellers alike. For airlines and airports, it means aligning their services and offerings with the evolving needs of their clientele. For travellers, it means making informed decisions that prioritize their safety, comfort, and overall satisfaction.

This dashboard seeks to address this critical intersection between passenger preferences and the aviation industry's strategies. By providing a comprehensive visualization that unravels these shifts over time, we empower travellers with invaluable insights to make well-informed decisions and develop contingency plans when booking their next flight. In a world where air travel plays an increasingly integral role, this research serves as a beacon, guiding both industry stakeholders and passengers towards a more seamless and tailored travel experience.

Passenger Satisfaction


Passenger Satisfaction Dashboard
View Our Analysis
Passengers’ satisfaction is crucial in providing improvements to airlines, leading to better service and efficiency. A dashboard is thus generated to provide in-depth analysis based on age and passenger segment.

From the Pie Chart Matrix, Business Class passengers show a high level of satisfaction across different flight categories. Notably, 77.80% of Business Class passengers on long-haul flights report being satisfied.

In contrast, Economy Class passengers, especially those on long-haul flights, appear to be the least satisfied. A significant 90.32% of Economy Class passengers on long-haul flights report being neutral or dissatisfied. This may be attributed to the extended duration of long-haul flights in Economy Class, potentially leading to passenger discomfort.

From the treemap, passengers aged 40 to 49 make up the bulk of the passengers’ and are generally satisfied. While passengers aged 60 to 69 is of a smaller group size, majority of the passengers within this age group are neutral or dissatisfied. Airlines may consider looking into improving the needs of passengers in this age group, given that this senior citizens’ age group might have different set of passenger needs compared to passengers from the younger group.

Piecing the charts together, we noted that passengers aged 40-49 are the biggest age group in general. This age group is also the biggest group of 90.32% neutral or dissatisfied passengers from the earlier analysis. This finding is significant as the size of this age group means that their level of satisfaction or dissatisfaction can significantly influence the overall passenger satisfaction rating. Therefore, airlines should prioritize addressing the needs and concerns of this specific passenger segment.

Notably, the average carrier delay minutes declined in 2020, reflecting the pandemic's influence, but increased in 2021 and 2022 as travel demand rebounded. Overall, the dashboard offers a detailed perspective on airline operational performance, regional variations in delays, temporal trends, and the factors influencing flight delays in the United States.

Conclusion

The analysis of our visualisation had identified the following opportunities for aviation industry to enhance their services and optimise operations strategies:

Improving Economy Class Offerings and Maintenance in June and July
In the period of June to July on a yearly basis, the visualisation depicts a substantial increase in the number of Economy Class bookings. The Passengers Satisfaction dashboard had also supported that this passenger segment is least satisfied on short and medium haul flights. By focusing on improving the quality of Economy Class, during this peak travel period, airlines may strategically leverage it to enhance the experience of this large customer base, leading to an increase in passengers’ overall rating.

Family-Centric Travel Packages for Popular Destinations
On top of the increase in Economy Class Bookings in June and July, it was also observed that there is a substantial increase in family travellers. In response, airlines may create family packages for flights to popular destinations. The packages will not only help airlines streamline its booking process, but also greater control in standardising in-flight services and amenities provided. By reducing the need for individualised requests, airlines can increase efficiency and deliver a consistent, high-quality travel experience to families, leading to an increased customer satisfaction.

Taking these insights into consideration will provide opportunities for airlines to plan out operational strategies, adapt and evolve to meet the changing needs of travellers, creating a memorable, and satisfying flying experience.
